Essais sur la vision du futur dans les films

In the realm of science fiction, robotics often takes center stage, captivating audiences with portrayals that straddle the line between imagination and reality. In the cyberpunk genre, robotics becomes a focal point, offering a vision of the future where advanced technology blurs the line between man and machine. Examining prominent examples from films like "Blade Runner" and "Ghost in the Shell," it becomes evident that the representation of robotics in these narratives is not entirely realistic but serves as a captivating exploration of the potential consequences of technological advancement.

If we delve into the portrayal of humanoid robots, such as the replicants in "Blade Runner," one must acknowledge the cinematic exaggeration for dramatic effect. While these creations possess remarkable human-like qualities. If robotics were to advance to such a degree, ethical concerns surrounding artificial intelligence and autonomy might arise, presenting potential challenges that need to be addressed.

Moreover, the amalgamation of man and machine in "Ghost in the Shell" raises intriguing possibilities but also prompts questions about the feasibility of such integration. The notion that individuals "should" or "ought to" enhance themselves with cybernetic components introduces ethical dilemmas that, if realized, would necessitate careful consideration and regulation.

In broader terms, when considering the representation of robotics in various movie genres, it becomes apparent that while these depictions offer imaginative glimpses into possible technological futures, the realism of such portrayals remains speculative.

Essais sur le transhumanisme

Transhumanism Is it a grace or a poisoned gift ?

the word “transhumanism” has been used for the first time by Julian Huxley, a well-known biologist, Firs director of the Unesco and founder of the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) in 1957: who said

“ The human species can, if it wishes, transcend itself […]; Perhaps transhumanism will serve: man remaining man, but transcending himself, by realizing new possibilities of and for his human nature. ”

So what does it mean ?

Wikipedia present Transhumanism as a philosophical movement, the proponents of which advocate and predict the enhancement of the human condition by developing and making widely available sophisticated technologies able to greatly enhance longevity, mood and cognitive abilities.

But this is too scientific, let me explain it my way :

several years ago a man who lost his leg will replace it by a wood made leg,

few years ago he will replace it by a realistic looking leg made of plastic,

today he will replace it by a sophisticated and fully functional bionic leg that look and act exactly like a normal leg

but what about a future where this artificial leg will exceed the real one so that every human will willingly, and even if he have his two functional leg, change his biological leg with an artificial one ?

And. In this exemple we are just speaking about a leg but what about other members ? What about a lung that will make you breath under water ? What about an eye that make you see over kilometers away or a back that will be so strong that you will be able to lift a car with no trouble ?

A world where inspector gadget will juste be considered as an average person.

it's crazy isn't it ? but not that crazy

this desire to acquire new capacities is not new.

As early as 18th and 19th centuries, we begin to trace the idea that human organism can be improved using technology. Philosophers, politicians, scientists of the Enlightenment already show an ardent desire to expand mankind’s capacities. Charles Darwin with its “Origin of Species”, Friedrich Nietzsche with the concept of “the overman” and even Mary Shelley whith “Frankenstein”

They all suggest the possibility of technological transformation of humankind and explore how these changes could alter the human condition.

Later on, in the middle of WWI, WWII and the postwar era, transhumanist ideas in writings still provoked fascination. In fact, many people found a new hope of a societal change and genuinely considered that progress in space travel, computers and medicine offered a prospect of a better world.

In particular Aldous Huxley, Brother of Julian Huxley, who wrote Brave New World in 1932 and where he describe a dystopian society where humans are created in laboratories.

By the way, fun fact, Andy Warhol once said that he wanted to be a machine, and that it would be a lot easier to be a machine because if something broke, you could just replace it.

So why are people so reluctant to accept the transhumanist movement?

Actually, several reasons can possibly explain that

First, the ethical way:

And one of the most actual and critical ethical reason concerns data privacy.

Overall, with the development of new technologies,

and it is even truer regarding the Internet, it becomes a real challenge to keep personal information private.

The transhumanist technologies are, by definition, intrusive in a person’s life at the highest level since they aim to be part of the human body. Consequently, it is relevant to consider the question of data protection when using these technologies.

And the most controversial issue about data privacy comes from a person’s health profile.

Data about health is quite sensitive and private.

To some extension, we can imagine that insurance companies or pharmaceutical groups might be eager to have access to these files, especially the ones regarding genetic predisposition about illnesses or DNA sequencing.

Another obstacle to the development of transhumanist technologies is the question of rising inequalities in a world where wealthy people can afford modifications to their body so they can run faster than cars without breaking a sweat. and where a normal person will just walk.

Nevertheless, it is easy for contradictors to pick an example such as mobile phones to oppose to that idea of inequalities :

By saying that twenty years ago we believed that cell phones were going to be used only by politicians or businessmen. Nowadays almost everyone owns one, even in one of the most underdeveloped countries they have cell phones.

Indeed technology also enables access to drinkable water, gives us the shoes that we wear and the food that we eat .

but one thing is sure about technologies, they have always generated more and more inequalities.

Second, the question of affordability and availability of potential transhumanist technologies:

as transhumanist tech might become a type of goods provided by companies to the public. In this situation, a risk, as well as a business opportunity, would be to monetize transhumanist tech and transform it into yet another product of modern capitalist society, making it a profitable business,

putting it forward as a “hype” and therefore forgetting the purpose of transhumanism.

Like remain us Gabriel Dorthe Doctor in Environmental Humanities at University Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne

“ It is not because you get a microchip under the skin that you can consider yourself as a transhumanist. ”

And even with that, What would be the modalities and regulations surrounding the commercialization of such products? This question remains, for the moment, unanswered.

So in the end is it a grace or a poisoned gift ?

I’ll just answer that without any prior knowledge on the subject, it would be easy to be scared by the transhumanist movement and their vision of the future.

this fantasy comes from all the dystopian content we have heard, saw and read, modern or contemporary.

(trust me after seeing the terminator for the first time) it is perfectly normal to be afraid of what is new, unknown and could possibly get out of our control but still, we should not make the mistake of mixing everything up.

Transhumanism, as a philosophical movement, is still very small and isolated,

and the development of new scary or dystopian-like technologies (such as the facial recognition in China) should not be linked to the beliefs of this group.

The truth is that you have, on one hand, the scientific and engineering part of the subject that develops new technologies in a free and unrestricted way. On the other hand, you find a few groups of thinkers that are eager to reach a point where the humankind will be able to upload someone’s brain on a computer or where we will create the Human 2.0 without really being too worried about what it implies in the short and middle-term. In between those two worlds, lots of questions arise

and the best science-fiction authors and film producers (as well as the journalists) have always known how to play with these dystopian fantasies.

and I’ll end on this quote from Gabriel Dorthe

“ The definition of technology generates significant misunderstandings because we never know very well what we want to talk about when we refer to it. ”
